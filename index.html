Channel spatial mapping time-frequency network for ambisonic-to-binaural audio rendering

Changjun He , Wenjie Zhang , Lianyu Zhou , Shiyun Xu , Mingjiang Wang* , Weiping Chen*
Harbin Institute of Technology, Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen)
*Corresponding Author
Virtual Reality

Abstract

Spatial audio delivers immersive auditory experiences by conveying spatial awareness and realism, making it a key component in applications such as virtual reality (VR), augmented reality (AR), and other AI-driven interactive systems. Ambisonics, as a widely adopted spatial audio format, often requires ambisonic-to-binaural rendering for headphone playback. While traditional DSP-based rendering methods suffer from numerical artifacts due to HRTF truncation, recent deep learning approaches still face challenges in accurately modeling interaural time difference (ITD) and interaural level difference (ILD). To address this, we propose a deep learning-based channel spatial mapping time-frequency network for ambisonic-to-binaural rendering. Specifically, we analyze ambisonics in the time-frequency domain and introduce a differential spatial attention module to extract directional cues via self-attention. We further design a gated spatial feature extraction module and a channel spatial attention mechanism to effectively capture and map spatial features to the binaural domain. To validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, we conduct quantitative and perceptual evaluations on the BTPAB dataset and compare it with other advanced methods. The experimental results show that our method can accurately render binaural audio from ambisonics and achieves state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance in both ITD (0.036 ms) and ILD (0.985 dB).
