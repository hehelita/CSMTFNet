Abstract

Spatial audio is widely applied in various scenarios, such as virtual and augmented reality, due to its unique spatial perception and realism. Among the methods of spatial audio reproduction, binaural audio is particularly favored for its convenience. In recent years, deep learning-based binaural audio synthesis methods have been explored to enhance the spatial quality of binaural audio. However, existing approaches continue to face challenges in phase estimation and the binauralization of out-of-distribution audio. To address these limitations, this paper proposes a novel binaural audio synthesis UNet (BASUNet). Specifically, we employ a time-domain UNet as the primary framework and introduce a multi-level position attention neck (MLPAN) module to integrate the sound source's orientation and position, effectively capturing its movement. Additionally, we incorporate a position fusion (PF) module, which further fuses the sound source's position and orientation during the upsampling and decoding process. Experimental results on the Binaural Speech Dataset demonstrate that the proposed BASUNet can synthesize accurate binaural audio and achieve state-of-the-art performance in phase estimation, improving by 3.0% compared to the best existing approach. Furthermore, BASUNet exhibits strong performance in binauralizing audio from out-of-distribution domains.
